 in this paper we present wavelm which learns universal speech representations from massive unlabeled speech data and adapts effectively across various speech processing tasks. investigation of the forward-forward algorithm has only just begun and there are many open questions. we propose a masked speech denoising and prediction framework for wave-lm where some inputs are simulated noisy overlapped speech with masks and the targets are produced the pseudo-label of the original speech on the masked region similar to hubert. can forward-forward produce produce a predictive model of images or video that is good enough to create the negative data needed for unsupervised loss. what is the best activation box to use? the backbone is a transformer encoder with l blocks. so far, only values have been explored from the unit. during pre-training, the transformer consumes masked acoustic features u and outputs hidden states hl.
